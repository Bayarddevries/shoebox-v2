Mabel's story

Mabel Starmer was a proud mother, and a grandmother. Her life was rooted in the land and communities of northern Manitoba in places like Churchill, The Pas, and Prince Albert. But her final journey was marked by a heartbreaking injustice.

While traveling by train with her daughter Louise, Mabel began to suffer from what we now know was a brain aneurysm. Disoriented and in pain, she cried out for help. But instead of receiving care, she was dismissed as a “drunk Indian” and locked in the baggage car by train staff.

By the time the train reached Brandon, it was too late. Mabel died shortly after arriving at the hospital. The hospital sent a bill to her husband charging him for care she never received. He refused to pay, believing their mistreatment had cost Mabel her life.

Years later, Mabel’s son returned to Brandon and paid the bill, just so the family could settle the estate and move forward.

Mabel's story is unfortunately one of many that illustrate the history of discrimination and inhumane treatment faced by countless Métis and Indigenous women.
